Chapter 4: Understanding Division

What is Division?

Division is one of the four basic mathematical operations. It is the process of splitting a number into equal parts or groups. Division is the opposite operation of multiplication.

Key Terms and Symbols

  • Dividend: The number being divided
  • Divisor: The number we divide by
  • Quotient: The answer or result of division
  • Remainder: What's left over when division doesn't result in a whole number
12 ÷ 3 = 4

In this example: 12 is the dividend, 3 is the divisor, and 4 is the quotient.

Division Symbols

Division can be written in several ways:

  • 12 ÷ 3 = 4 (division symbol)
  • 12 / 3 = 4 (forward slash)
  • 12 ÷ 3 = 4 (horizontal line)

Real-Life Example

Sarah has 15 cookies and wants to share them equally among 3 friends. How many cookies will each friend get?

15 ÷ 3 = 5

Each friend will get 5 cookies.

Basic Division Facts

Division by 1

Any number divided by 1 equals itself.

  • 5 ÷ 1 = 5
  • 10 ÷ 1 = 10
  • 100 ÷ 1 = 100

Division by the Same Number

Any number (except zero) divided by itself equals 1.

  • 7 ÷ 7 = 1
  • 25 ÷ 25 = 1
  • 143 ÷ 143 = 1

Division by Zero

Important Rule: Division by zero is undefined. You cannot divide any number by zero.

For example, 8 ÷ 0 has no answer because there's no number that, when multiplied by 0, gives 8.

Relationship with Multiplication

Division and multiplication are opposite operations. We can check our division by multiplying:

If 12 ÷ 3 = 4, then 4 × 3 should equal 12

Check: 4 × 3 = 12 ✓

Long Division Method

Long division is a method for dividing large numbers. It follows a simple pattern: Divide, Multiply, Subtract, and Bring down.

Steps in Long Division:

  1. Divide: How many times does the divisor go into the first digit(s) of the dividend?
  2. Multiply: Multiply the quotient digit by the divisor
  3. Subtract: Subtract the result from the dividend
  4. Bring down: Bring down the next digit
  5. Repeat: Continue until all digits are used

Example: 84 ÷ 4

      21
   ________
4 | 84
    8    ← 4 × 2 = 8
    --
    04   ← 8 - 8 = 0, bring down 4
     4   ← 4 × 1 = 4
    --
     0   ← 4 - 4 = 0

Therefore, 84 ÷ 4 = 21

Example with Remainder: 85 ÷ 4

      21 R1
   ________
4 | 85
    8     ← 4 × 2 = 8
    --
    05    ← 8 - 8 = 0, bring down 5
     4    ← 4 × 1 = 4
    --
     1    ← 5 - 4 = 1 (remainder)

Therefore, 85 ÷ 4 = 21 remainder 1

Checking Your Work

To check long division with remainders, use this formula:

(Quotient × Divisor) + Remainder = Dividend

For our example: (21 × 4) + 1 = 84 + 1 = 85 ✓

Division with Decimals

Dividing Decimals by Whole Numbers

When dividing a decimal by a whole number, place the decimal point in the quotient directly above the decimal point in the dividend.

Example: 6.8 ÷ 4

      1.7
   ________
4 | 6.8
    4     ← 4 × 1 = 4
    --
    28    ← 68 - 4 = 28 (bring down 8)
    28    ← 4 × 7 = 28
    --
     0

Therefore, 6.8 ÷ 4 = 1.7

Dividing by Decimals

When the divisor is a decimal, make it a whole number by moving the decimal point to the right. Move the decimal point in the dividend the same number of places.

Example: 12.6 ÷ 1.8

Step 1: Move decimal points

1.8 becomes 18 (moved 1 place right)

12.6 becomes 126 (moved 1 place right)

Step 2: Divide normally

126 ÷ 18 = 7

Therefore, 12.6 ÷ 1.8 = 7

Converting Fractions to Decimals

Division can be used to convert fractions to decimal form:

  • 1/2 = 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5
  • 1/4 = 1 ÷ 4 = 0.25
  • 3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
  • 1/8 = 1 ÷ 8 = 0.125

Division Word Problems

Strategies for Solving Word Problems

  1. Read carefully: Understand what the problem is asking
  2. Identify key information: What numbers are given? What do you need to find?
  3. Look for keywords: "each," "per," "equally," "share," "group"
  4. Set up the problem: Write the division equation
  5. Solve and check: Calculate and verify your answer makes sense

Types of Division Problems

1. Equal Groups (Measurement Division)

Problem: Maria has 24 stickers. She wants to put them in groups of 6. How many groups can she make?

Solution: 24 ÷ 6 = 4 groups

2. Equal Sharing (Partition Division)

Problem: There are 35 students going on a field trip. If they travel in 5 buses with equal numbers of students in each bus, how many students are in each bus?

Solution: 35 ÷ 5 = 7 students per bus

3. Rate Problems

Problem: A car travels 240 miles in 4 hours. What is the car's average speed per hour?

Solution: 240 ÷ 4 = 60 miles per hour

4. Cost per Unit

Problem: A pack of 12 pencils costs $3.60. How much does each pencil cost?

Solution: $3.60 ÷ 12 = $0.30 per pencil

Challenge Problems:

  1. A recipe calls for 2.5 cups of flour to make 10 cookies. How much flour is needed for each cookie?
  2. If 144 books are arranged equally on 6 shelves, and each shelf has 4 rows, how many books are in each row?
  3. A farmer harvested 1,250 apples. If he packs them in boxes of 25 apples each, how many boxes will he need?
